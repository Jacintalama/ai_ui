# Remotion Best Practices

Guidance the AIUI video generator follows when you choose Animated mode. It steers the **plan** — the story, motion, pacing, and narration. AIUI renders that plan with a deterministic Remotion composition. Edit this to change how your videos are directed.

## Mental model
- A video is a function of time. Think in **beats measured in seconds**: every scene has an entrance, a hold, and an exit. Motion must have **intent** — never constant movement for its own sake.
- Motion is **frame-driven** (fade, slide, scale, zoom), eased so it reads as deliberate, not mechanical. The screenshot is the **hero**; motion choreographs *around* it.

## Structure (arc)
- Open with a short **title** beat: what it is and why it matters (~2–2.5s).
- Then **2–4 screenshot** beats on the strongest features or benefits. Use only the best shots; skip weak or repetitive ones.
- Close with a short **outro / call-to-action**.

## Motion — choose per beat (zoom-in, zoom-out, pan-up, pan-left, rise, fade)
- Match the motion to the beat's job: **zoom-in** to focus one feature, **pan-up / pan-left** to reveal long content, **rise / fade** for text beats.
- **Vary it** — do not reuse one motion every scene, and avoid a run of identical motions. Entrances should feel like they *arrive*; exits like they *leave*.

## Pacing (in seconds — frame rate is chosen at render time)
- 2.5–5s per scene. Vary the lengths; avoid a run of identical durations.
- Keep it tight: **20–35s ideal, 40s hard cap**.

## Headlines & subtext
- Punchy and benefit-led, **≤ ~8 words**. Say *why it matters* — never read the UI verbatim.
- Optional subtext: one short supporting line.

## Narration
- Conversational, **one idea per scene**, speakable within the scene's duration (~2.5 words per second). Write one flowing script for the whole video.

## Hard rules
- Reference **only** the provided screenshot filenames, exactly as given, and only on screenshot scenes.
- Keep total duration under 40 seconds.

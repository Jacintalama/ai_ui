Our Story

Born from a family table in Tuscany

Founded in 2014 by chef Marco Ferretti and his partner Elena, <%= APP_NAME %> began as a thirty-seat dining room tucked behind a flower market. Marco trained under the Michelin-starred kitchens of Florence before moving to the city, carrying with him his grandmother's hand-rolled pasta recipes and an unwavering commitment to the wood-fired hearth. From the first night — a sold-out soft opening with a four-course menu scratched on a chalkboard — guests felt the warmth that has defined the restaurant ever since.

A decade on, the philosophy remains rooted in seasonal sourcing and honest technique. The menu changes every eight weeks in step with what local growers and foragers bring to the back door. You might find wild-nettle pappardelle in March, heirloom tomato bruschetta in August, and slow-braised short rib with white truffle in November. Every dish is cooked to order over applewood and olive-branch embers in the open kitchen — a theatre that anchors the room and fills every corner with the scent of the countryside.

A Rare Victorian on One of Brookline's Most Coveted Streets

This extraordinary 1890 Victorian at 42 Maple Street presents a singular opportunity to own a fully restored piece of Brookline history. Set behind a wrought-iron gate on a deep lot shaded by century-old maples, the home retains every original detail — intricate millwork, wide-plank hardwood floors, soaring 10-foot ceilings, and paired bay windows — while offering entirely contemporary systems and finishes. The result is a home that feels at once anchored in the past and perfectly suited to modern life.

A complete kitchen renovation completed in 2022 introduced Calacatta marble countertops, custom shaker cabinetry in a warm sage, a professional 36-inch gas range, and a pot-filler above the island. The primary suite occupies the entire third floor, featuring a walk-in dressing room, spa bath with heated floors, and a private reading alcove overlooking the garden. Two additional bedrooms share a renovated hall bath. The property also includes a finished basement suitable as a home office or gym, an attached one-car garage, and a professionally landscaped rear garden with a cedar deck — rare for in-town Brookline.

Brookline is one of Greater Boston's most desirable communities — a walkable, tree-lined town that combines the energy of the city with the quiet confidence of an established residential neighborhood. Maple Street sits just four blocks from Coolidge Corner, the vibrant village center anchored by independent boutiques, the landmark Coolidge Corner Theatre, and celebrated restaurants ranging from neighborhood bistros to James Beard-recognized kitchens. The Green Line at Coolidge Corner station puts downtown Boston only 15 minutes away.

Families are drawn to Brookline for its award-winning public schools — consistently ranked among the top districts in Massachusetts — as well as its network of parks, running paths along the Muddy River, and abundant recreational facilities at the Larz Anderson Auto Museum grounds. The community also enjoys convenient access to Route 9, the Longwood Medical Area, and the campuses of Harvard and Boston University, making this address equally appealing to professionals and families alike.

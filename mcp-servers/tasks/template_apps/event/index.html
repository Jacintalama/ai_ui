Meet the Speakers

Forty-two speakers. Twelve headline acts. Every talk is selected for depth, originality, and real-world impact.

Maya Patel

VP Engineering, Northbeam
Maya leads platform engineering at Northbeam, where she scaled their data infrastructure to process 10 billion events per day. She speaks on distributed systems and org design.

Hiroshi Tanaka

Distinguished Engineer, Atlas
Hiroshi is the co-designer of Atlas's globally distributed consensus protocol. His 20-year career spans compilers, operating systems, and real-time analytics at scale.

Aisha Mohammed

CTO, Halcyon Labs
Aisha built Halcyon's ML platform from the ground up, pioneering edge-deployed inference long before it became mainstream. She is a vocal advocate for responsible AI deployment.

Luca Rossi

Head of Infrastructure, Meridian
Luca has run infrastructure for Meridian Cloud's multi-region deployments serving 40 million users. He speaks on Kubernetes at the edge and cost-efficient cloud architecture.

Sofia Chen

Principal Researcher, Cortex AI
Sofia's research on sparse transformer architectures has been cited over 4,000 times. At Cortex AI she leads the team pushing the boundaries of efficient large-model training.

James Okoye

Engineering Director, Vantage
James leads a 120-person engineering organization at Vantage. His talks on engineering culture and shipping velocity have helped thousands of teams move faster without burning out.

Rafael Torres

Founder, Omnilux
Rafael founded Omnilux after shipping developer tooling at three previous startups. He is one of the loudest voices in the open-source observability community.

Priya Sharma

Staff Engineer, Aurora Networks
Priya re-architected Aurora's routing protocol handling 2 trillion packets per day. She writes prolifically about network engineering, correctness, and formal verification.

Daniel Weiss

CEO, Solace Technologies
Daniel started Solace in a Berlin co-working space and grew it to a 200-person company with no outside funding. He speaks candidly about bootstrapping, pricing, and founder mindset.

Marcus Klein

Open Source Lead, Northwind

Nia Adeyemi

Eng. Manager, Blackbird
Nia leads product engineering at Blackbird, where she shaped the hiring and onboarding process that reduced ramp time by 40%. She speaks on inclusive engineering practices.

Carlos Rivera

Principal Architect, Halcyon
Carlos designed Halcyon's zero-downtime deployment pipeline that handles 800 production deploys per day. He is passionate about making reliability engineering accessible to small teams.

Where We Gather

The Venue

Tempodrom Arena  ·  Möckernstrasse 10, 10963 Berlin, Germany

The Tempodrom Arena is one of Berlin's most distinctive event spaces — a sweeping, tent-inspired structure set on the banks of the Spree. Originally erected for an alternative cultural festival in the 1980s, it has since been rebuilt in permanent form and now houses a 3,500-seat main hall with state-of-the-art AV infrastructure, three breakout rooms, and a sun-drenched glass atrium ideal for networking. The venue is fully wheelchair accessible and has underground parking for 400 vehicles.

For the three days of the conference, the entire west wing will be dedicated to sponsor exhibition booths, a curated bookshop, and a barista bar serving specialty coffee from Berlin roasters. The main auditorium seats 1,500 comfortably, with overflow viewing available in the atrium via live broadcast screens. Evening socials on both days will take place in the landscaped courtyard, weather permitting.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Tempodrom Arena has an underground car park with capacity for 400 vehicles, available at standard Berlin city rates. We recommend arriving by public transport: the venue is a 3-minute walk from Anhalter Bahnhof S-Bahn station and a 5-minute walk from the U-Bahn at Mendelssohn-Bartholdy-Park.
Catered lunches on both days include clearly labelled vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and halal options. When registering, please use the dietary requirements field to let us know about allergies or preferences, and we will ensure your needs are met.
All main-stage keynotes and selected breakout sessions will be professionally recorded and made available to all ticket holders within three weeks of the event. Access is provided via a private streaming portal linked from your confirmation email. Workshop sessions are not recorded out of respect for participant privacy.
Full refunds are available until August 1, 2026. Between August 1 and September 1 a 50% refund is available. After September 1 tickets are non-refundable but fully transferable — contact us at tickets@conference.example to transfer your registration to a colleague or friend at no additional charge.
The conference operates under a strict Code of Conduct. We are committed to a welcoming, harassment-free environment for attendees of all backgrounds. A full copy of the code is provided at registration and displayed at the venue. A dedicated safety team is on-site throughout both days; anyone who experiences or witnesses a violation should report it to a staff member wearing a teal lanyard, or via the anonymous reporting link in your attendee portal.
Tempodrom Arena is fully wheelchair accessible with step-free entry, accessible restrooms on every level, and reserved seating at the front of all session rooms. Live captioning is provided for all main-stage talks. If you have specific accessibility requirements — sign language interpretation, sensory needs, or anything else — please contact us before August 1 and we will make arrangements.
